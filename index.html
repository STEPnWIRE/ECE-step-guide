Structure your answers using STAR:

Task: Describe your goal.
Action: Explain what you did.
Result: Share the outcome.

A Day in the Life of a STEP Intern

Ever wonder what it's really like to be a STEP intern? This section provides a glimpse into the daily experience, from the impactful projects you'll tackle to the unique mentorship structure that ensures you learn and grow every day.

Impactful Projects

Work on real Google products, contributing to code that affects millions of users. Projects are designed for learning and are integrated into Google's core services, spanning areas like Search, Android, and Google Classroom.

Unique Mentorship

You'll work in a pair with another intern, guided by two experienced Google engineers ("Hosts"). This structure fosters collaboration and provides a strong support system for technical and professional growth.

Growth & Development

Your days will include coding, team meetings, 1:1s with your mentors, and attending tech talks by Google leaders. It's a dynamic environment focused on expanding your skills and professional network.

Step 1: Find the Opening

The primary channel is the official Google Careers website. Applications for summer internships typically open in the fall of the preceding year. Set up alerts and check frequently. Also, engage with Google's campus outreach programs.

Step 2: Prepare Your Documents

  • Resume: One page, highlight projects, skills, and coursework. Quantify achievements.
  • Transcript: An unofficial or official transcript is required.
  • Cover Letter: Optional but highly recommended. Tailor it to Google and the STEP role.

Step 3: Apply Early

The application process is highly competitive, and positions fill up quickly on a rolling basis. Submitting your application as soon as the portal opens significantly increases your chances of being considered.

Step 4: The Interview Stage

After an initial online assessment, you'll face technical and behavioral interviews. Technical rounds focus on DSA, while behavioral rounds assess "Googleyness". After passing interviews, you enter a project matching phase with different teams.

Step 5: Receive an Offer

If a team selects you during project matching, and you pass the final review, you'll receive an official offer! This includes details about your team, location, and stipend.
